Module 1

CV & Resume Building

📄 ChemE CV Structure

A strong chemical engineering CV highlights technical skills, quantified achievements, and relevant project experience. Here's the winning structure:

  • Header: Name, LinkedIn, GitHub, location, email
  • Summary: 3-line technical profile (role, skills, impact)
  • Education: Degree, GPA if strong, relevant coursework
  • Experience: Internships, co-ops, research positions
  • Projects: Design projects, final year project, simulations
  • Skills: Software, lab techniques, languages
  • Publications/Awards: Papers, scholarships, competitions

💬 Writing Powerful Bullet Points

Each bullet point should follow the CAR formula: Challenge → Action → Result.

  • Weak: “Worked on distillation column design”
  • Strong: “Designed a 15-stage distillation column for ethanol-water separation using Aspen Plus, reducing energy consumption by 18% vs. base case”

Module 2

Software Skills Roadmap

The tools that make you a more employable and effective chemical engineer.

🐍

Python for ChemE

Python is the most versatile programming language for ChemE: data analysis, simulations, ML, and automation.

  • NumPy & SciPy for numerical methods
  • Pandas for process data wrangling
  • Matplotlib for process visualization
  • Cantera for thermochemical calculations
Free Open source
⚙

Aspen Plus & HYSYS

Industry-standard process simulation tools used in oil & gas, petrochemicals, and specialty chemicals worldwide.

  • Steady-state and dynamic simulation
  • Property package selection (NRTL, PR, SRK)
  • Sensitivity analysis and design specs
  • Free student license available via AspenTech
Student license
📈

MATLAB

Widely used in academia and R&D for numerical computation, control system design, and signal processing.

  • ODE solvers for reactor modelling
  • Control toolbox for PID and state-space
  • Simulink for dynamic systems
  • Free via MATLAB Online for students
📄

AutoCAD & CAD Tools

P&ID drawing and equipment layout design using industry-standard CAD platforms.

  • AutoCAD P&ID for piping diagrams
  • SmartPlant / AVEVA E3D for 3D plant design
  • Free AutoCAD student licence available
🌏

COMSOL & CFD

Computational Fluid Dynamics for transport phenomena, reaction engineering, and multiphysics simulation.

  • COMSOL Multiphysics for coupled simulations
  • OpenFOAM (free, open-source CFD)
  • ANSYS Fluent for turbulent flows
📊

Excel & Power BI

Don't underestimate Excel — still the #1 tool in industrial ChemE. Power BI adds professional data dashboards.

  • Advanced formulas, Solver, Goal Seek
  • VBA macros for process calculations
  • Power BI for process KPI dashboards

Scholarships & Research Navigation

🎓 MSc & PhD Scholarship Strategy

Securing a postgraduate scholarship requires a targeted strategy combining academic excellence, research alignment, and compelling applications.

  • Step 1: Identify research groups aligned with your interests (use Google Scholar, ResearchGate)
  • Step 2: Email supervisors directly with a focused research proposal (2 pages max)
  • Step 3: Apply to Chevening, Commonwealth, Erasmus+, DAAD, and government scholarships
  • Step 4: Build GPA and publications during undergrad to strengthen applications

🔎 Finding the Right Research Supervisor

Choosing a supervisor is the most important decision in your postgraduate career. Be strategic and proactive.

  • Use Google Scholar to find supervisors publishing in your area
  • Read 2-3 of their recent papers before emailing them
  • Reference specific papers in your email to show genuine interest
  • Ask about funding, timeline, and group culture in your first meeting
  • Check ResearchGate, LinkedIn, and university pages for contact details

💼 Job Search Strategy for ChemE

Chemical engineering graduates can pursue diverse careers. Knowing where to look and how to position yourself is key.

  • Target industries: Oil & gas, petrochemicals, pharma, food & beverage, mining, water treatment, energy
  • Job boards: LinkedIn, Indeed, IChemE Jobs, AIChE Career Center, local government portals
  • Networking: IChemE/AIChE student chapters, LinkedIn outreach, university career fairs
  • Graduate programs: Apply to structured 2-year graduate engineer programs at major companies

🔧 Essential Soft Skills

Technical skills get you the interview. Soft skills get you the job and the promotion.

  • Technical communication: presenting process designs and data to non-engineers
  • Project management: understanding of PMBOK, Gantt charts, critical path
  • Team leadership and cross-functional collaboration
  • Report writing: clear, concise technical reports for industry audiences
  • Risk assessment mindset: always asking “what could go wrong?”
