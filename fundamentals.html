ChemE Fundamentals

The mathematical, thermodynamic, and chemical foundations every chemical engineer must master.

Module 1

Engineering Mathematics

The quantitative toolkit underpinning all chemical engineering analysis.

∫ Differential Equations

Chemical engineering problems frequently involve rates of change. ODEs describe how concentrations, temperatures, and pressures evolve over time or along a reactor.

  • First-order linear ODEs: dy/dx + P(x)y = Q(x)
  • Separation of variables for simple rate equations
  • Systems of ODEs for multi-component balances
  • Numerical methods: Euler, Runge-Kutta (RK4)
dy/dt = ky  →  y(t) = y₀ ekt

∑ Linear Algebra & Matrices

Multi-component systems require solving simultaneous equations. Matrix methods are essential for process simulation and control.

  • Gaussian elimination and LU decomposition
  • Eigenvalues for stability analysis
  • Least squares regression for data fitting
  • Matrix operations in MATLAB and Python (NumPy)
Ax = b  →  x = A-1b

∂ Partial Differential Equations

Distributed parameter systems like heat exchangers and packed beds require PDEs for full spatial-temporal description.

  • Parabolic PDEs: transient heat and mass transfer
  • Elliptic PDEs: steady-state diffusion problems
  • Hyperbolic PDEs: wave propagation in pipes
  • Finite difference and finite element methods
∂T/∂t = α ∂²T/∂x²

📊 Statistics & Data Analysis

Engineers interpret experimental data, fit models, and design experiments using statistical tools.

  • Probability distributions: normal, Poisson, binomial
  • Hypothesis testing and confidence intervals
  • Design of Experiments (DoE) and ANOVA
  • Regression analysis and error propagation
R² = 1 - SSres/SStot
Module 2

Thermodynamics

Energy, entropy, equilibrium, and the laws that govern all physical and chemical processes.

△ The Four Laws

  • Zeroth Law: Thermal equilibrium and temperature measurement basis.
  • First Law: Energy conservation: dU = δQ - δW
  • Second Law: Entropy of an isolated system never decreases: dS ≥ 0
  • Third Law: Entropy approaches zero as temperature approaches absolute zero.

📈 Phase Equilibria & VLE

Vapour-liquid equilibrium is central to distillation and absorption design.

  • Raoult's Law: yᵢP = xᵢPᵢsat
  • Henry's Law for dilute solutions
  • Equations of state: ideal gas, van der Waals, Peng-Robinson
  • Activity coefficients: NRTL, UNIQUAC, Wilson models
G = H - TS  |  ΔG° = -RT ln K

⚙ Gibbs Free Energy & Equilibrium

Spontaneity and equilibrium are governed by the minimization of Gibbs free energy at constant T and P.

  • Chemical potential and fugacity concepts
  • Equilibrium constants and Le Chatelier's principle
  • Effect of temperature: van't Hoff equation
  • Effect of pressure on gas-phase equilibria
d(ln K)/d(1/T) = -ΔH°/R

🔥 Combustion & Energy Cycles

Power generation, furnace design, and environmental analysis require combustion thermodynamics.

  • Standard heats of formation and reaction
  • Adiabatic flame temperature calculation
  • Carnot, Rankine, and Brayton cycles
  • Efficiency limits and irreversibility
ηCarnot = 1 - Tcold/Thot
Module 3

Mass & Energy Balances

The foundation of every process calculation: what goes in must come out (or accumulate).

⚖ Mass Balance Framework

For any control volume under steady-state conditions:

[In] - [Out] + [Generation] - [Consumption] = [Accumulation]
  • Tie components and recycle stream calculations
  • Reactive systems with extent of reaction
  • Multi-unit process flowsheets
  • Degrees of freedom analysis

🔆 Energy Balance Framework

For open systems with flow work (shaft work) and heat transfer:

ΔḢ = Q̇ - Ẇs
  • Sensible heat: Q = m·Cp·ΔT
  • Latent heat for phase changes
  • Heat of reaction contributions
  • Combined mass and energy balance problems
