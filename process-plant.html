Process & Plant Design

From reaction kinetics to full plant flowsheets, process control, and industrial safety — engineering at scale.

Module 1

Reaction Engineering

⚒ Reaction Kinetics

Rate laws describe how fast chemical reactions proceed, forming the basis for reactor sizing and selectivity optimization.

  • Elementary vs. non-elementary rate laws
  • Rate constant temperature dependence (Arrhenius)
  • Reaction order determination from experimental data
  • Catalytic reactions: Langmuir-Hinshelwood mechanism
-rA = k CAn

🏭 Reactor Design: CSTR, PFR, PBR

Three ideal reactor models form the foundation of chemical reaction engineering design.

  • CSTR (Batch-mixed): V = FA0X / (-rA)
  • PFR (Plug flow): V = FA0 ∫ dX/(-rA)
  • PBR (Packed bed): W = FA0 ∫ dX/(-r'A)
  • Levenspiel plots for graphical reactor sizing

🔥 Selectivity & Yield

Multiple reactions compete for reactants. Maximizing selectivity to the desired product drives reactor choice and operating conditions.

  • Instantaneous selectivity SD/U = rD/rU
  • Overall selectivity and yield analysis
  • Series vs. parallel reaction networks
  • Temperature and concentration effects on selectivity

🔆 Non-ideal Flow & RTD

Real reactors deviate from ideal plug flow or perfect mixing. Residence Time Distribution (RTD) analysis quantifies mixing behaviour.

  • E(t) and F(t) curves from tracer experiments
  • Tanks-in-series and dispersion models
  • Mean residence time and variance
  • Conversion predictions using RTD data
τ = V / v0 = CA0X / (-rA)exit
Module 2

Process Flowsheets & P&IDs

📄 Process Flow Diagrams (PFDs)

PFDs show the major equipment items, process streams, and conditions at a high level for conceptual process design.

  • Equipment representation: reactors, columns, exchangers
  • Stream tables with flow rates, temperatures, pressures
  • Material and energy balance integration
  • Simulation tools: Aspen Plus, HYSYS, ProII
Aspen Plus HYSYS ProII

⚙ Piping & Instrumentation Diagrams (P&IDs)

P&IDs add instrumentation, control loops, and safety devices to the PFD, forming the engineering basis for construction.

  • ISA standard symbols for instruments and valves
  • Control loops: PID controllers, cascade control
  • Safety instrumented systems (SIS) and interlocks
  • P&ID review in HAZOP studies
Module 3

Process Control & Safety

📊 Process Control Fundamentals

Feedback and feedforward control maintain process variables at setpoints despite disturbances.

  • PID controller: Proportional, Integral, Derivative actions
  • Transfer functions and Laplace transforms
  • Bode plots and stability analysis (gain/phase margins)
  • Ziegler-Nichols tuning methods
Gc(s) = Kc[1 + 1/(τIs) + τDs]

⚠ HAZOP & Process Safety

Hazard and Operability (HAZOP) studies systematically identify process deviations and their consequences.

  • HAZOP guide words: No, More, Less, Reverse, Other Than
  • Bow-tie analysis for barrier identification
  • Layer of Protection Analysis (LOPA)
  • Safety Integrity Level (SIL) determination
HAZOP LOPA SIL

💰 Plant Economics & Costing

Engineering decisions must be evaluated on economic grounds using capital and operating cost estimates.

  • Capital cost estimation: Lang and Guthrie methods
  • Operating costs: utilities, raw materials, labour
  • Net Present Value (NPV) and Internal Rate of Return (IRR)
  • Sensitivity analysis and break-even analysis
NPV = ∑ CFt / (1+r)t

🏭 Scale-up & Commissioning

Moving from lab to pilot to full-scale plant introduces challenges that require systematic engineering approaches.

  • Dimensional analysis and scale-up rules
  • Pilot plant design and data collection
  • Pre-startup safety review (PSSR)
  • Commissioning, start-up, and performance testing
