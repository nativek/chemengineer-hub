Essential ChemE Equations

The most important relationships every chemical engineer must know.

△ First Law of Thermodynamics

Energy cannot be created or destroyed — only converted between forms. For an open system:

dU/dt = Q̇ - Ẇ + ∑(Fᵢ · Hᵢ)

Where Q is heat flow, W is work, F is molar flow, and H is enthalpy.

🌊 Continuity Equation (Mass Balance)

For a steady-state system with no accumulation:

∑(mass in) = ∑(mass out)

Forms the basis of all process flowsheet calculations.

🔆 Hagen-Poiseuille (Pipe Flow)

Laminar flow pressure drop through a cylindrical pipe:

ΔP = 128μLQ / (πd⁴)

Fundamental to pump sizing and piping design.

🔥 Arrhenius Equation (Reaction Kinetics)

Temperature dependence of reaction rate constants:

k = A · e(-Ea/RT)

Critical for reactor design and understanding catalysis.
