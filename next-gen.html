Machine learning, data science, digital twins, and sustainable engineering reshaping the chemical industry.

Module 1

Machine Learning for Chemical Engineers

🤖 ML Fundamentals for ChemE

Machine learning enables engineers to build predictive models from process data without needing explicit physical equations.

  • Supervised learning: regression (property prediction), classification (fault detection)
  • Unsupervised learning: clustering, anomaly detection
  • Feature engineering from process sensor data
  • Cross-validation and model evaluation metrics (RMSE, R², F1)
Python scikit-learn TensorFlow

📈 Process Optimization with ML

Data-driven optimization can find operating conditions that maximize yield, minimize energy, or reduce waste beyond what first-principles models can achieve alone.

  • Gaussian Process Regression (GPR) for surrogate models
  • Bayesian optimization for expensive-to-evaluate processes
  • Neural networks as universal function approximators
  • Reinforcement learning for adaptive process control

📊 Data Science Workflow

A structured workflow ensures reproducibility and reliability when applying data science to engineering problems.

  • Data collection: process historians (OSIsoft PI, Aspen InfoPlus)
  • Data cleaning: handling missing values, outliers, sensor drift
  • Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA): correlation heatmaps, PCA
  • Model deployment: REST APIs, Docker containers

🔅 Natural Language Processing for Engineers

NLP tools help engineers extract insights from safety reports, patents, research papers, and maintenance logs at scale.

  • Text classification of incident reports and near-misses
  • Patent mining for competitive intelligence
  • Large Language Models (LLMs) for engineering Q&A
  • Automated literature review and summarization

Sustainable & Green Engineering

🌿 Green Chemistry Principles

The 12 Principles of Green Chemistry guide the design of products and processes that reduce or eliminate hazardous substances.

  • Atom economy: maximize incorporation of reactants into products
  • Safer solvents and auxiliaries
  • Renewable feedstocks and bio-based chemicals
  • Catalysis over stoichiometric reagents
Atom Economy = (MW of desired product / MW of all products) × 100%

♻ Circular Economy & Life Cycle Analysis

Moving from a linear take-make-dispose model to circular systems requires quantitative tools like LCA to measure environmental impact.

  • Life Cycle Assessment (LCA): goal, scope, inventory, impact
  • Carbon footprint calculation and reduction strategies
  • Process intensification: microreactors, reactive distillation
  • Waste valorization and by-product synergies

⚡ Hydrogen Economy & Energy Transition

Green hydrogen, electrolysis, and power-to-X technologies are reshaping energy-intensive industries.

  • Electrolysis efficiency: alkaline, PEM, solid oxide
  • Hydrogen storage: compressed, liquid, metal hydrides
  • Ammonia as hydrogen carrier (Haber-Bosch 2.0)
  • Carbon capture, utilization, and storage (CCUS)

🔣 Bio-based & Pharmaceutical Processes

Fermentation, biocatalysis, and biorefinery concepts are expanding chemical engineering into life sciences.

  • Fed-batch and continuous fermentation design
  • Downstream processing: centrifugation, chromatography
  • Bioreactor scale-up: kLa and mixing time correlations
  • cGMP manufacturing and quality by design (QbD)
