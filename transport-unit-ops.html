Transport & Unit Operations

Fluid flow, heat and mass transfer, and separation processes that form the core of chemical plant design.

Module 1

Fluid Mechanics & Flow

🔄 Fluid Statics & Dynamics

Understanding pressure, flow regimes, and pipe flow is essential for pump selection, pipe sizing, and safety relief design.

  • Reynolds number: Re = ρvD/μ (laminar <2100, turbulent >4000)
  • Bernoulli equation for ideal flow: P/ρ + v²/2 + gz = constant
  • Darcy-Weisbach equation for pipe friction losses
  • Moody chart: friction factor vs. Reynolds number
Re = ρvD / μ

⚒ Pumps, Compressors & Valves

Mechanical equipment selection and sizing requires understanding pump curves, NPSH, and compressor work.

  • Centrifugal vs. positive displacement pumps
  • Net Positive Suction Head (NPSH) requirements
  • Pump curve and system curve intersection
  • Compression work: isentropic and polytropic
Wpump = ṁ ΔP / (ρ η)
Module 2

Heat Transfer

🔥 Three Modes of Heat Transfer

  • Conduction: q = -k A (dT/dx) — Fourier's Law
  • Convection: q = h A (Ts - T∞) — Newton's Law of Cooling
  • Radiation: q = σεA(T1&sup4; - T2&sup4;) — Stefan-Boltzmann
Q = U A ΔTlm

⚙ Heat Exchangers

Shell-and-tube, plate, and double-pipe heat exchangers are designed using the LMTD or NTU-effectiveness methods.

  • Log Mean Temperature Difference (LMTD) method
  • NTU-effectiveness method for unknown outlet temps
  • Overall heat transfer coefficient U calculation
  • Fouling factors and their impact on design area
ΔTlm = (ΔT1 - ΔT2) / ln(ΔT1/ΔT2)
Module 3

Mass Transfer & Separation Processes

📈 Diffusion & Mass Transfer Coefficients

Mass transfer drives separations like distillation, absorption, and extraction. Fick's laws describe molecular diffusion.

  • Fick's First Law: J = -D (dC/dz)
  • Film theory and mass transfer coefficients
  • Analogy between heat and mass transfer (Sh, Nu, Re, Sc, Pr)
  • Two-film resistance model for gas-liquid systems
NA = ky(yA - yA*)

🌍 Distillation

Distillation is the most widely used separation operation in the chemical industry, separating components based on relative volatility.

  • McCabe-Thiele graphical method for binary systems
  • Minimum reflux ratio (Underwood equation)
  • Fenske equation for minimum theoretical stages
  • Murphree tray efficiency
αAB = (yA/xA) / (yB/xB)

💧 Absorption & Stripping

Gas-liquid contacting for removing soluble components from gas streams or stripping volatile species from liquids.

  • Operating and equilibrium line construction
  • Height of a Transfer Unit (HTU) and NTU
  • Kremser equation for stage counting
  • Packed vs. tray column selection criteria
HOG = HG + (mGM/LM)HL

⚖ Drying, Crystallization & Membranes

Solid-liquid separations and novel membrane processes are critical in pharmaceuticals, food, and water treatment.

  • Constant-rate and falling-rate drying periods
  • Nucleation and crystal growth kinetics
  • Reverse osmosis: solution-diffusion mechanism
  • Membrane selectivity and permeability
